# Hokkaido + Tokyo Itinerary — Draft v1

**Dates:** July 14–30, 2026 (17 days / 16 nights)
**Routing:** LAS → HND → CTS (arrive Sapporo) ... CTS → HND → Tokyo → HND/NRT → LAS
**Pace:** Mostly relaxed with light activity sprinkled in

---

## Route Overview

```
Sapporo (3 nights)
  └─ Otaru day trip (Day 3)
  → Furano / Biei (2 nights) — peak lavender
  → Jozankei (1 night) — private ryokan + onsen
  → Sapporo Beer Garden (midday stop)
  → Asahikawa (1 night) — ramen
  → Daisetsuzan / Sounkyo (2 nights) — gorge + ropeway
  → Shiretoko (2 nights) — UNESCO wilderness
  → Lake Akan (1 night) — private ryokan + Ainu culture
  → Kushiro (1 night) — wetlands + market
  → [HAKODATE — TBD, see Day 14]
  → Tokyo (2 nights) — Cocon dinner + souvenirs
  → Fly home
```

## Day-by-Day

### Day 1 — Monday, July 14 — Arrive Sapporo

**Travel:** Land CTS → train/bus to Sapporo (~40 min)
**Accommodation:** Royal Park Canvas ($156) or Tokyu Stay Sapporo Odori ($150)

- Easy arrival day — long flight from LAS, keep it simple
- Check in, freshen up, walk Susukino
- **Dinner:** Ramen Yokocho (Ramen Alley, Susukino) — try Sapporo miso ramen to start the trip right

---

### Day 2 — Tuesday, July 15 — Sapporo

**Accommodation:** Same Sapporo city hotel

- **Breakfast:** Nijo Market (Nijo Ichiba) — kaisen-don; opens early
- **Morning:** Shiroi Koibito Park → ROYCE Chocolate Town (chocolate factory tours, exclusive products)
- **Afternoon:** Chitosetsuru Sake Museum (free tastings) → Sapporo Beer Museum (historic brick brewery)
- **Optional detour:** Makomanai Takino Cemetery — Moai stone heads + Tadao Ando's "Hill of the Buddha" emerging from a lavender field; one of Sapporo's most surprising hidden gems
- **Evening:** Soup curry dinner — Soup Curry Garaku (the "quiet champion," tucked in an alley) or Aiiro
- After dinner: Walk Susukino entertainment district; Okaeri Izakaya for drinks if energy allows

---

### Day 3 — Wednesday, July 16 — Otaru Day Trip

**Accommodation:** Sapporo city hotel (last night)

- **Train to Otaru:** 30–40 min from Sapporo
- **Morning:** Otaru Canal and Sakaimachi Street — brick warehouses, glassblowing studios, music boxes
- **Lunch:** Seafood on Sakaimachi — fresh uni or crab
- **Afternoon (choose one):**
  - Nikka Whisky Yoichi Distillery (30 min from Otaru) — historic distillery, free museum, tastings ★
  - Aoyama Villa — Meiji-era herring baron's mansion; beautifully preserved, few tourists
- **Before the train back:** Kita no Aisu soft serve stand (near the canal, open since 1978) — velvety Hokkaido milk ice cream
- Return to Sapporo by evening

---

### Day 4 — Thursday, July 17 — Sapporo → Furano

**Accommodation:** Furano area hotel/guesthouse

- **Option A:** Rental car pick-up in Sapporo; scenic drive to Furano (~2 hrs)
- **Option B:** Furano-Lavender Express train from Sapporo (seasonal; check schedule)
- **Afternoon:** Shikisai no Oka (Hill of Colors) — curated flower fields in horizontal color rows; great photography even on the first day
- **Evening:** Furano town dinner; Hokkaido dairy products, melon soft serve
- Note: Lavender is in peak bloom right now — perfect timing

---

### Day 5 — Friday, July 18 — Furano & Biei — Peak Lavender ★★

**Accommodation:** Furano/Biei area (second night)

> 🌸 Sapporo Summer Festival opens today — we'll catch it on Day 7

- **7–8am (early!):** Farm Tomita — arrive before the tour buses; the most famous lavender farm in Japan; free entry; also has poppies, marigolds, sunflowers all in bloom
- **Mid-morning:** Lavender East (Kamifurano) — one of Japan's largest lavender fields; far less crowded than Farm Tomita
- **Afternoon:** Biei scenic drive
  - Patchwork Road — rolling hills of differentiated crop fields; quilt-like effect; best by car
  - Biei Blue Pond (Shirogane Blue Pond) — vivid turquoise/blue pond; one of Japan's most-photographed spots; beautiful at dusk
- **Optional:** Furano-Biei Norokko Train for part of the route — slow panoramic tourist train with lavender décor
- **Evening:** Stay in Furano or Biei; lavender soft serve is mandatory

---

### Day 6 — Saturday, July 19 — Furano → Jozankei Ryokan ★

**Accommodation:** Jozankei Onsen — **Yurakusoan** ($370–$580) or **Kasho Gyoen** ($580–$850)
*Both have private in-room rotenburo. Book via IKYU.com 3–5 months ahead.*

- **Morning:** Any remaining Furano/Biei spots (Blue Pond at dawn is stunning)
- **Midday:** Drive back toward Sapporo (~2 hrs)
- **Detour:** Moerenuma Park — designed entirely by Isamu Noguchi; surreal landscape sculptures; worth 1–2 hrs
- **Late afternoon:** Check in at Jozankei Onsen (~50 min south of Sapporo)
  - Yurakusoan: private in-room cedar onsen + 4 free kashikiri baths (rock, ceramic, hinoki, hiba); free post-bath ramen
  - Kasho Gyoen: private forest-view rotenburo; Michelin-recognized kaiseki
- **Evening:** Multi-course kaiseki dinner at the ryokan; long soak in your private outdoor bath
- *The Sapporo Summer Festival Beer Garden (~50 min away) is optional tonight if you have the energy — or save it for tomorrow*

---

### Day 7 — Sunday, July 20 — Jozankei → Sapporo Beer Garden → Asahikawa

**Accommodation:** Asahikawa city hotel

- **Morning:** Ryokan breakfast + morning onsen soak (the best way to start a day)
- **Midday:** Drive to Sapporo (~50 min); **Odori Park Summer Festival Beer Garden** ★
  - 10,000+ seats across 7 blocks; multiple brewery zones; food: zangi, Genghis Khan, grilled corn; runs 12:00–21:00
  - This is one of Japan's largest outdoor beer events — don't miss it
- **Afternoon:** Drive to Asahikawa (~1.5 hrs from Sapporo)
- **Evening:** Asahikawa Ramen Village (Ramen Mura) — 8 legendary local shops; try the shoyu ramen with its distinctive lard float
  - Ask for Ramen Shishio specifically — a 15-seat hidden dive in the area; incredible

---

### Day 8 — Monday, July 21 — Asahikawa → Daisetsuzan (Sounkyo)

**Accommodation:** Sounkyo Onsen — **Sounkyo Choyotei** ($200–$400) or **Hotel Taisetsu**

- **Morning (optional):** Asahiyama Zoo — Japan's most innovative zoo; famous immersive enclosures; good if you enjoy wildlife
- **Midday:** Drive to Sounkyo Onsen (~1.5 hrs)
- **Afternoon:** Sounkyo Gorge walk — flat, easy path through towering columnar basalt cliffs; Ryusei and Ginga waterfalls visible from the trail
- **Optional:** Sounkyo Ropeway up to Kurodate plateau (~30 min ride; easy walking at the top)
- **Evening:** Gorge-view onsen; kaiseki dinner at the ryokan

---

### Day 9 — Tuesday, July 22 — Asahidake Ropeway + Alpine Wildflowers ★

**Accommodation:** Sounkyo ryokan (second night)

- **Morning:** Drive to Asahidake (~1 hr from Sounkyo); Asahidake Ropeway up to ~1,600m
- **Easy hike:** Sugatami Pond loop (1–2 hrs, no steep climbing) — above the treeline; alpine wildflowers in full bloom in late July (primula, Aleutian avens, crocus-like blooms); Hokkaido's highest peak looms above
- No hiking experience needed; this is the ropeway + a gentle high-altitude stroll
- **Afternoon:** Return to Sounkyo; Momijidani Trail (flat, 60–90 min along the gorge river) if energy allows
- **Evening:** Onsen and a quiet dinner at the ryokan

---

### Day 10 — Wednesday, July 23 — Sounkyo → Shiretoko (Utoro)

**Accommodation:** Utoro area hotel (Shiretoko)

- Long driving day (~3–3.5 hrs); stop at Memanbetsu area or Abashiri for lunch if needed
- **Afternoon:** Furepe Falls ("Tears of a Maiden") — 20-min flat forest trail; falls drop directly from a cliff into the sea; no crowds
- **Evening:** Fresh seafood dinner in Utoro — you're in some of Japan's best seafood territory now
- Note: Pick up rental car in Sounkyo or Asahikawa if switching vehicles

---

### Day 11 — Thursday, July 24 — Shiretoko ★★

**Accommodation:** Utoro hotel (second night)

- **Morning:** Shiretoko Five Lakes — elevated wooden boardwalk (800m, fully accessible, no guide needed by late July); views of the lakes with the Shiretoko mountain range behind; keep eyes open for brown bears near the treeline
- **Midday:** Kamuiwakka Hot Falls ★ — scramble up a river of naturally hot spring water cascading down a hillside; wade and soak in natural rock pools; one of Japan's most unusual experiences
- **Afternoon:** Shiretoko coastal wildlife cruise (1.5–2 hrs) — boat tour along the inaccessible Shiretoko coastline; spot Steller's sea eagles, seals, and brown bears on shore ★
- **Evening:** Seafood dinner; Utoro is known for excellent uni and crab

---

### Day 12 — Friday, July 25 — Shiretoko → Lake Akan ★★

**Accommodation:** **Akan Tsuruga Bessou Hinanoza** ($400–$600) — all 25 suites have private outdoor onsen; Michelin recommended; book IKYU.com 3–5 months ahead

- **Morning:** Shiretoko Pass (Shiretoko横断道路) — dramatic mountain road crossing the spine of the peninsula; views of both the Sea of Okhotsk and the Pacific; often still snowbanked on the sides in summer
- **Drive to Lake Akan (~3 hrs)**
- **Afternoon:** Ainu Kotan village ★ — the largest Ainu indigenous village in Japan; artisan craft shops, cultural performances, traditional food; the most authentic place in Hokkaido to engage with Ainu culture
- **Evening:** Marimo exhibition aquarium (the rare spherical algae are a national natural monument); kaiseki dinner at Hinanoza; soak in your private outdoor onsen overlooking the lake

---

### Day 13 — Saturday, July 26 — Akan-Mashu Lakes Loop

**Accommodation:** Kushiro city hotel

- **Morning:** Slow ryokan breakfast; morning private onsen on the lake
- **Caldera lakes loop (drive):**
  - **Lake Mashu** — observation deck views of Japan's clearest lake (often draped in dramatic fog — both clear and foggy are stunning in different ways)
  - **Lake Kussharo / Sunayu** — Japan's largest caldera lake; at Sunayu beach, dig a hole in the sand and hot spring water fills it naturally; soak in your own improvised outdoor onsen ★
  - **Lake Onnetō** (hidden gem) — tiny color-shifting caldera lake at the base of Mt. Meakan; changes between blue-green, jade, and silver; almost no tourists
- **Evening:** Drive to Kushiro (~1.5 hrs); dinner at a local izakaya

---

### Day 14 — Sunday, July 27 — Kushiro

**Accommodation:** See decision point below

- **Morning:** Kushiro Wetlands canoe tour ★ — paddle the Kushiro River through Japan's largest wetland; relaxed and scenic; realistic chance of seeing tancho cranes and Ezo sika deer from water level
- **Midday:** Washo Market ★ — "do-it-yourself" kaisendon; buy a bowl of rice from the market entrance and pile it with uni, ikura, and crab from different vendor stalls; unique experience
- **Afternoon:** HAKODATE DECISION POINT

---

### ⚑ HAKODATE — Decision Needed

**Option A — Include Hakodate (recommended if visiting):**
- Afternoon: Drive or fly CTS → Hakodate (~4–5 hrs by car; or fly via CTS ~1.5 hrs)
- Night 14: Hakodate hotel
- **Day 14B (Jul 28):**
  - 5am: Hakodate Asaichi Morning Market ★ — live squid fishing + immediate preparation; the most atmospheric market experience in Hokkaido
  - Morning: Fort Goryokaku + Goryokaku Tower (star fortress aerial view)
  - Afternoon: Motomachi historic district (consulate buildings, churches) + Hakodate Park Ferris Wheel (Japan's oldest operating)
  - Evening: Mt. Hakodate night view by ropeway ★ (top 3 night views in Japan — go on a clear night)
  - **Exit:** Shinkansen from Shin-Hakodate-Hokuto → Tokyo (~4 hrs) — arrives at Tokyo Station; no need to fly
- Night 15–16: Tokyo

**Option B — Skip Hakodate:**
- Afternoon Jul 27: Fly CTS → HND (1hr 45min, ~$77–$150 on ANA/JAL)
- Arrive Tokyo with more time; slower pace for 2 Tokyo nights
- Night 14–16: Tokyo (3 nights instead of 2, or use the extra day for a Tokyo day trip)

---

### Day 15 — Monday, July 28 — Arrive Tokyo

**Accommodation:** Tokyo hotel — Nakameguro (walking distance to Cocon), Ginza, or Shibuya

- **Arrive:** Via CTS → HND flight (~1hr 45min) OR shinkansen from Hakodate → Tokyo Station (~4 hrs)
- **Afternoon:** Check in; decompress
- **Hokkaido Dosanko Plaza** (Yurakucho, near Ginza) ★ — official Hokkaido government shop; 1,200+ Hokkaido-exclusive products; Royce Nama Chocolate, Rokkatei Marusei Butter Sand biscuits, rotating limited-edition products; open until 10pm
- **Evening: Dinner at Cocon** ★★
  - Address: 4F Chiyo Nakameguro Building Annex, 3-8-3 Kami-Meguro, Meguro-ku (1–2 min from Nakameguro Station)
  - Chef trained at Mirazur (3 Michelin stars, France) + Bennelong at Sydney Opera House; French technique with Modern Australian + Japanese seasonal ingredients; natural wine pairing
  - ~¥11,000/person food only; ~¥15,000–¥20,000+ with wine pairing
  - **Book via Pocket Concierge** 2–4 weeks ahead; Cocon is closed Wednesdays
  - Counter-seated, intimate; smart casual dress

---

### Day 16 — Tuesday, July 29 — Tokyo — Souvenir Day

**Accommodation:** Tokyo hotel (last night)

- **Morning: Asakusa** — traditional souvenir shopping
  - Nakamise-dori shopping street (Japan's oldest; 89 shops, 250m leading to Sensoji Temple)
  - Look for: Edo Kiriko cut glass, fans, tenugui, ceramics, Ningyo-yaki sweets
  - Funawa Honten (imo-yokan sweet potato jelly, open since 1902)
  - Ame-shin amezaiku candy sculpting — uniquely Tokyo, very giftable
- **Afternoon: Ginza** — high-end gifts
  - Ginza Six (underground food hall for premium packaged sweets)
  - Ginza Mitsukoshi (department store; impeccably wrapped Japanese food gifts)
  - Shiseido Parlour — exclusive Hanatsubaki Biscuit tin (only available at this Ginza flagship)
- **Evening:** Tokyu Hands Shibuya (stationery, kitchenware, practical Japanese gifts) or Don Quijote (affordable bulk snacks and cosmetics, open late); dinner in the neighborhood
- **Pack tonight** — long flight tomorrow

---

### Day 17 — Wednesday, July 30 — Fly Home

- Morning: Last Tokyo breakfast
- **Tokyo Station Gransta** — great last-minute souvenir spot if flying from Narita (N'EX from Tokyo Station)
- Head to **HND** (Haneda) or **NRT** (Narita) for LAS flight
  - HND: Tokyo Monorail from Hamamatsucho (~20 min, ¥500); or Keikyu Line from Shinagawa
  - NRT: Narita Express N'EX (~60 min from Tokyo Station, ¥3,070) — add extra time buffer
