## Notes

### Cape Kamui
- The trailhead parking lot fills up on weekends in July — arrive before noon if possible.
- The cliff walk is ~2.4km round trip, mostly flat boardwalk. Takes 40–50 min at a leisurely pace.
- **Shakotan Blue:** The sea color here is a distinct cobalt blue in summer — one of the most photogenic spots in Hokkaido.
- Views extend to the cape tip rock formation. On clear days, you can see the Shakotan coastline curving south.

### Shakotan Uni Lunch
- Shakotan is one of Hokkaido's top uni (sea urchin) production areas. July–August is peak season for fresh bafun uni and murasaki uni.
- Port-side restaurants in Shakotan-cho and Bikuni are the spots — look for places with uni-don (uni rice bowl) on the board out front.
- Expect ¥3,000–6,000/person for a proper uni bowl set.

### Otaru
- **Check-in:** Most canal-area hotels are 5–10 min walk to the canal and Sakaimachi Street.
- **Canal golden hour:** The Otaru Canal lanterns light up at dusk. Summer sunset is ~7:30pm — arrive at the canal by 6:30pm for the best progression from daylight to lanterns.
- **Sakaimachi Street:** Glass studios and music box shops close around 6–7pm. If you want to browse the Otaru Glass Museum or KITAICHI Glass, aim to be there by 6pm.
- **Dinner options:** Otaru has excellent sushi (Masazushi, Nishiya), izakaya near the canal, and ramen. Sushi restaurants can have queues on Sunday evenings — check wait times on arrival or make a reservation.

### Drive Notes
- Niseko → Cape Kamui: ~1.5 hrs, mostly coastal Route 229. Scenic drive with sea views opening up past Iwanai.
- Cape Kamui → Otaru: ~1.5 hrs continuing along Route 229 / Route 5 into Otaru. Easy highway section at the end.
- Total driving today: ~3 hrs. No single leg is long.
