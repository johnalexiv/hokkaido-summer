## Park Hyatt Niseko Hanazono ⭐ Draft v2 Pick — Nights 1–2

- ~$500–800/night; 100 rooms across tower and chalets; Mt. Yotei and ski slope views
- **Amenities:** Full-service spa + indoor pool; breakfast buffet 7–10:30am (sashimi station, noodle station, grilled salmon, eggs to order); multiple dining outlets
- **Summer activities on-site:** Hanazono Zipline (Asia's largest mega-zip — MACH3 course 1.7km, up to 110 km/h); Tree Trekking (7 high-ropes courses through birch forest); e-bike rentals; golf at Hanazono 18-hole course (May–Nov)
- **Nearby:** Shiribetsu River rafting (~15 min); Kutchan town e-bike loop; Niki town fruit picking
- Book direct at park.hyatt.com or via Hyatt app; 3–6 months ahead for July
- **Booking note:** Confirm summer package rates; sometimes includes breakfast + activity voucher

---

## Hoshino Resorts KAI Poroto ⭐ Draft v2 Pick — Night 9

- ~$300–500/night for two with dinner + breakfast; lakeside setting on Lake Poroto
- **Onsen:** Rare "moor spring" (植物性モール温泉) — dark brown, organic-rich water from ancient plant matter; therapeutic properties differ from sulfur or iron springs; private baths + communal baths
- **Ainu theme:** Architecture inspired by Ainu motifs; craft activity included (make Ikema plant amulets); Ainu-style dishware for meals
- Adjacent to **Upopoy National Ainu Museum** — plan for morning after check-in; reserve museum entry online in advance (¥1,200)
- Dinner: Hokkaido seafood — crab, botan shrimp, scallops, salmon
- **Evening option from property:** Jigokudani Onibi no Michi (illuminated steam path); Demon Fireworks (Oni-hanabi) Thu/Fri nights June–August at 8:30pm — 8-meter fire columns + taiko drums ★
- Book via hoshinoresorts.com (English available); popular, book 3–4 months ahead

## Lake Toya Tsuruga Resort Hikari no uta ⭐⭐ Draft v2 Pick — Nights 10–11

- ~$400–700/night for two with meals; opened April 2023; 48 rooms, all with private open-air baths overlooking Lake Toya
- **Dining flexibility** (major differentiator): choose between French, kaiseki, or teppanyaki each evening — all Hokkaido ingredients; no obligation to do pure kaiseki every night
- **Fireworks:** Lake Toya nightly fireworks run late April–October at 8:45pm (~450 fireworks, 20-min show); visible from rooms and hotel terrace ★★
- Hillside garden setting; part of the Tsuruga Group (same operator as Hinanoza at Lake Akan)
- Book via hikarino-uta.com (English) or Jalan/IKYU; opened 2023 so still relatively bookable; 3–5 months ahead for July
- **Room note:** Confirm "open-air bath" (露天風呂付) when booking — all 48 rooms have one but verify at time of reservation

## Private Ryokan — By Area

> All prices are per night for two guests with two meals (dinner + breakfast) included — the standard ryokan "ippaku nishoku" model — unless noted.

### Jozankei (50 min from Sapporo — most accessible)

**Okujozankei Kasho Gyoen** ⭐ Top Pick for Jozankei
- ~$580–$850/night for two with meals
- Every room has a private onsen opening to Hokkaido forest; indoor and outdoor options
- Michelin Guide recognized; guests describe food as "Michelin star quality kaiseki" featuring seasonal Hokkaido ingredients
- Small property, boutique feel; rated 9.2/10
- Best booking channel: IKYU.com; book 3–6 months ahead

**Jozankei Onsen Yurakusoan**
- ~$370–$580/night for two with meals
- Private in-room cedar tub onsen in all rooms PLUS four free kashikiri (reservable private) baths in different materials: hiba wood, cedar, stone, ceramic — most properties charge for kashikiri
- Free extras: post-bath ramen, ice pops, sake tasting, indoor fireplace lounge
- More modern aesthetic; 5-star service ratings consistently
- Available on Booking.com and IKYU; 2–4 months ahead for summer

**Jozankei Daiichi Hotel Suizantei** (already on your radar at $367/night)
- At $367 you are likely in a standard room without a private outdoor bath; private rotenburo rooms (6th/7th floor) run $500–$700+
- Top-tier **Touyu room**: private open-air bath + large private sauna + 130-inch projector theater room
- **Seikaiha room**: private open-air bath + HARVIA CILINDRO sauna (sleeps up to 4)
- **Shokudo Ichi** restaurant is Michelin-starred; Chef Komatsu trained under French chef Michel Bras — a genuine culinary highlight
- Operating since 1957; the anchor ryokan of Jozankei

### Niseko / Kutchan

**Zaborin** ⭐ Best in Hokkaido
- ~$1,200–$2,000+/night per villa, meals included
- 15 standalone villas — each with TWO private onsen (one indoor, one covered open-air rotenburo fed by free-flowing natural spring water); total privacy
- **Michelin 5 Red Pavilions** (the top distinction); kaiseki dinners described as "worth three Michelin stars"; 10+ courses, fully different each night, local Hokkaido ingredients only
- Minimalist Japanese architecture; choice of tatami/futon or Western bed
- Summer is cheaper than winter ski season — July is a good value window relative to the property's peak pricing
- Book direct at zaborin.com (English) or The Ryokan Collection; 4–6 months minimum advance booking

---

### Lake Toya

**The Lake Suite Ko no Sumika**
- ~$270–$540+/night
- All 80 rooms: private open-air onsen + 9.5 sqm infinity terrace facing the lake
- Lake Toya fireworks run nightly late April–October (~8:55 PM, 20 min) — visible from your private terrace
- Primary channel: IKYU.com; book 3–4 months ahead for summer

**Lake Toya Tsuruga Resort Hikari no Uta** (opened April 2023)
- ~$270–$610+/night; 33 of 48 rooms have private open-air baths
- Unusually diverse dining: kaiseki, French, or teppanyaki — all using Hokkaido ingredients; major differentiator for those who don't want pure kaiseki every night
- Part of the Tsuruga Group (same operator as Hinanoza at Lake Akan)
- Easier to book than older prestige properties; 2–3 months ahead for summer

**WE Hotel Toya**
- All 55 rooms have cypress (hinoki) open-air baths; 100% private rotenburo per room
- Designed by **Kengo Kuma** (also designed Tokyo 2020 Olympic Stadium) — the building itself is an architectural destination
- Tattoo-friendly (rare for onsen properties in Japan)
- More relaxed, modern vibe vs. traditional ryokan

### Noboribetsu

**Takinoya**
- From ~¥77,000/night (~$480–$520) for two with meals
- 12 rooms with private open-air hot spring baths; kaiseki served in a private dining room overlooking a Japanese garden — not a shared dining room
- The most prestigious ryokan in Noboribetsu; Zen-inspired interiors, direct-source spring water (Noboribetsu has 11 types of mineral springs — the most in Japan)
- IKYU.com is the best booking channel; book 2–4 months ahead

**Bourou Noguchi Noboribetsu** (Adults Only)
- ~¥60,000–¥80,000/night (~$400–$540) for two with meals
- All suites 50 sqm+; every room has a private scenic-view bath using direct Noboribetsu spring water
- Japanese-Western fusion kaiseki with French influences; private dining rooms
- Adults-only — significantly more peaceful than mixed family hotels
- Free shuttle from JR Sapporo Station and New Chitose Airport
- Book direct at bourou.com (English) or Booking.com

### Hakodate (Yunokawa Onsen)

**Bourou Noguchi Hakodate**
- From ~¥38,650/person with meals (~$520+/night for two)
- Michelin 3 Pagoda distinction; multi-story duplex suite rooms with private onsen on private terraces
- Three room categories: SUITE (duplex with terrace rotenburo), WAMODERN (private bath, Hakodate aesthetic), OHITORISAMA (single-occupancy — rare)
- Yunokawa Onsen has a 350-year history; 10 min from the morning market and Mt. Hakodate
- Book direct at bourou-hakodate.com (English) or Booking.com

## Booking Tips

**Platforms (best to worst for ryokan):**
1. **IKYU.com** (ikyu.com/en-us) — Japan's premier luxury travel platform; best inventory for high-end ryokan; English interface; some exclusive room types only listed here
2. **Jalan.net** (jalan.net/en) — Japan's #1 accommodation site by volume; 3-month advance booking limit
3. **Rakuten Travel** (travel.rakuten.com) — large domestic inventory; earn Rakuten points; English available
4. **Booking.com** — most convenient for foreign travelers; decent inventory but sometimes misses top room categories
5. **Direct booking** — Zaborin and Bourou Noguchi both have strong English sites; direct booking can yield better packages or upgrades

**How far ahead to book:**
- Zaborin: 4–6 months minimum
- Kasho Gyoen, Hinanoza: 3–5 months for July
- Yurakusoan, Takinoya, Bourou Noguchi: 2–4 months
- Lake Toya properties: 2–3 months (Hikari no Uta is easiest as it's newer)
- Rule of thumb: rooms with private onsen sell before rooms without

**What to confirm:**
- Specify you want a room with a private outdoor bath (rotenburo) — not all rooms at every property have one; the price gap is significant
- Ask about kashikiri policies — free at Yurakusoan; at others typically ¥1,000–¥3,000 for a 45-min slot
- All properties above accept major international credit cards; smaller rural ryokan may prefer cash
