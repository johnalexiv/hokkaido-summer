# Hokkaido Hidden Gems

Lesser-known spots rarely on tourist itineraries, especially for foreign visitors.

---

## Shakotan Peninsula
**Near:** Otaru / Niseko (1.5 hrs from Sapporo by car)

One of Hokkaido's most dramatic coastal stretches, almost entirely missed by foreign tourists. **Cape Kamui** at the tip has electric-blue water ("Shakotan Blue") and towering white cliffs; a 20-minute trail leads to the point. Good snorkeling in July when the water clears. **Requires a car.** Combine with an Otaru day.

---

## Kaminoko Pond
**Near:** Kiyosato, eastern Hokkaido (car only)

A tiny spring-fed pond maintained at a near-constant 8°C year-round. The water is so clear that fallen trees and fish appear to float suspended in mid-air. Far less crowded than Biei's Blue Pond. Often called one of Japan's most beautiful small water bodies. Pair with a Shiretoko/eastern Hokkaido loop.

---

## Lake Onnetō
**Near:** Akan-Mashu National Park, base of Mt. Meakan

A color-shifting caldera lake that changes between blue-green, silvery gray, and jade depending on weather and minerals — sometimes multiple times in one day. Very few tourists make the side trip from Lake Akan. No entrance fee, short boardwalk trail. Combine with a day hiking on Mt. Meakan.

---

## Rishiri & Rebun Islands
**Access:** Ferry from Wakkanai (~2 hrs); Wakkanai is 5–6 hrs north of Sapporo by train
**Stay: 2–3 days minimum for both islands**

Two small islands forming Japan's northernmost national park (**Rishiri-Rebun-Sarobetsu National Park**). Feel genuinely remote; rarely visited by foreign tourists.

- **Rishiri Island** — dominated by the near-perfect cone of **Mt. Rishiri** (1,721m), a dormant volcano often compared to Mt. Fuji; hiking season officially opens in July. Sea urchin here (Rishiri uni) is considered among the best in Japan.
- **Rebun Island ("Island of Flowers")** — almost same latitude as Sakhalin, Russia; 300+ species of alpine flowers bloom at sea level due to the cold climate; the **8-hour coastal hiking trail (Hachijikan Course)** is one of Hokkaido's great wilderness walks; max elevation only 490m but terrain is wild and scenic. July is ideal for flowers.

**Note:** Adding these islands requires 2+ extra days and a significant detour to Wakkanai. Worth it if you prioritize remote nature over urban experiences.

## Akkeshi
**Near:** Kushiro (eastern Hokkaido coast)

A small fishing town famous for **Akkeshi oysters** — considered Japan's finest. Farmed to be excellent year-round, including summer (unusual — most Japanese oysters peak in winter). The town also has significant waterfowl diversity. Almost no tourists come here. Worth a meal stop if driving the eastern Hokkaido coast.

---

## Yubari
**Near:** Central Hokkaido (1.5 hrs from Sapporo by car/train)

Japan's most famous "bankrupt city" (officially declared bankruptcy in 2007; population has since dramatically shrunk) produces the world's most expensive melons. **Yubari King Melons** are grown under obsessive cultivation conditions. In July, you can visit farms and buy gift melons at more reasonable local-market prices. A poignant study in rural Japan's demographic challenges as well as a genuine food destination.

---

## Cape Nosappu (Nemuro)
**Near:** Nemuro, easternmost Hokkaido (very long drive from most places)

The easternmost point of Hokkaido — and of Japan's main territory — with views across the strait to Russian-administered islands. Genuinely end-of-the-world feeling. Nemuro town has exceptional seafood: famous for **Esan ramen** and extremely fresh crabs and scallops. Very few tourists. Best suited for someone who wants to see the real, non-tourist Hokkaido.

---

## Makomanai Takino Cemetery (Sapporo)
**Near:** South Sapporo (20 min by car from the center)

One of Sapporo's most surprising hidden spots — and not what the name suggests. The grounds are home to two extraordinary art installations: **giant Easter Island-style Moai heads** clustered on a hill, and architect **Tadao Ando's "Hill of the Buddha"** — a monumental buried white Buddha statue that emerges only from the shoulders up through a perfectly manicured lavender field, framed by a 40-meter earthen mound tunnel. The contrast is surreal. In July, the lavender surrounding the Buddha is in bloom. Almost no foreign tourists know about this place.

---

## Meiji Park Brick Silos (Nemuro)
**Near:** Nemuro, far eastern Hokkaido

Some of the largest and oldest surviving brick silos in Japan, set on a hilltop against sweeping natural scenery. Deeply photogenic in a decaying, melancholy way — very unusual for Japan where old structures are typically demolished. Combine with a visit to Cape Nosappu or the Akkeshi oyster stop.

---

## Kamikawa Taisetsu Sake Brewery (near Asahikawa/Daisetsuzan)

A small-scale artisan rural brewery that produces rare **Doburoku** — cloudy, unfiltered sake that's normally illegal for private breweries to make in Japan (this one has special dispensation). Tasting in a quiet, unpretentious farmhouse setting. Combines naturally with a Daisetsuzan or Asahikawa stay.

---

## Nikka Whisky Yoichi Distillery
**Near:** Yoichi (30 min from Otaru, 1 hr from Sapporo)

One of Japan's most historic whisky distilleries, founded in 1934 by Masataka Taketsuru (who trained in Scotland). Free museum, affordable tastings, and tours of the beautiful pagoda-kiln buildings. Easy to combine with an Otaru day trip. Far less crowded than Suntory Yamazaki.

---

## Sunayu (Lake Kussharo Beach Onsen)
**Near:** Akan-Mashu National Park

At Lake Kussharo — Japan's largest caldera lake — hot spring water seeps directly up through the sandy shoreline. You can dig a hole in the beach sand, let it fill with geothermally heated water, and soak in your own improvised private onsen. Free. Surreal. Combine with Lake Mashu and Lake Akan.
