## Park Hyatt Niseko: The Biggest Win

Two paths; both worth considering. **Act before May 2026** — Hyatt is repricing its award chart.

### Option A — Hyatt Points (Chase UR → Hyatt transfer, 1:1)

- Park Hyatt Niseko is **World of Hyatt Category 8**
- **Before May 2026 repricing:** ~40,000–45,000 pts/night
- **After May 2026 (July peak pricing):** likely 50,000–75,000 pts/night
- 2 nights = 100,000–150,000 Hyatt points → saves ~$1,000–1,600 cash
- Transfer Chase Ultimate Rewards → World of Hyatt at 1:1 on chase.com
- Award stays earn Hyatt elite night credit; can stack with status benefits

### Option B — Amex Fine Hotels & Resorts (FHR)

Park Hyatt Niseko Hanazono is a **confirmed FHR property** on Amex Travel.

Every FHR booking includes:
- Daily breakfast for two (~$60/room minimum per day → ~$120–180 value over 2 nights)
- $100 property credit (spa, F&B, or other eligible charges)
- Room upgrade on arrival (subject to availability)
- Guaranteed 4pm late checkout
- Early check-in when available
- Up to **$600/year back** on prepaid FHR bookings (semi-annual $300 Amex statement credit)

FHR net cost for 2 nights (at ~$600/night avg): ~$1,200 cash minus $600 credit offset minus ~$150+ breakfast value minus $100 property credit = **effective ~$350–450 out of pocket** for 2 nights at a Park Hyatt.

### Best Play

> Book one night on Hyatt points + one night via Amex FHR — capture both the points savings and the breakfast/property credit perks.

> If you have 100,000+ Hyatt points, pure points redemption likely wins on value. If points are short, FHR with the $600 annual credit makes cash booking far more palatable.

**Important:** FHR bookings are prepaid through Amex Travel and do not earn Hyatt stay/night credit toward elite status.

## Rental Car — July 16–27 (12 days)

**Use CSR exclusively. Decline the rental company's optional CDW.**

| Feature | CSR | Amex Platinum |
|---|---|---|
| Coverage type | **Primary** | Secondary |
| Limit | $75,000 | $75,000 |
| Requires personal insurance first | No | Yes |

- **Decline:** the optional "Menseki Hosho" (deductible waiver / CDW) at the counter — this is what CSR replaces
- **Keep:** the mandatory "Sharyo Hoken" (basic vehicle damage insurance included by Japanese law) — this is separate and required
- **Before the trip:** Call Chase Card Benefits and request a **written coverage letter** listing your name, dates, countries covered, and vehicle types — Japanese rental desks sometimes ask for proof
- **Savings vs. buying CDW:** ~$20–40/day × 12 days = **$240–480**
- Amex Platinum is secondary coverage in Japan — don't use it for the car

## Flights: Insurance vs. Earning

| Card | Earning on flights | Trip delay insurance |
|---|---|---|
| **Amex Platinum** | **5x MR** points | $500 per trip (shared) |
| **CSR** | **4x UR** points | **$500 per person** |

**Charge flights to CSR.** The per-person delay coverage ($500 each = $1,000 combined for two travelers) significantly outweighs the 1x earning gap (~$50–100 in points on a $3,000–5,000 RT ticket). On a transpacific trip with connections, delays are a real risk.

## Other Benefits

### Global Entry / TSA PreCheck
Both cards offer a **$120 credit** every 4 years (covers one Global Entry application, which includes TSA PreCheck). If neither traveler has Global Entry yet, use one card per person — one application per credit. Highly recommended for the long return from Japan.

### Foreign Transaction Fees
Both cards: **0% foreign transaction fees.** No surcharges on any purchases in Japan or Tokyo.

### Girlfriend's Card (CSR only)
- Own Priority Pass → free entry at LAS, HND, CTS lounges independently
- Own trip delay/cancellation coverage if flights charged to her CSR
