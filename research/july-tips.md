# July-Specific Tips for Hokkaido

## What's in Season

| Item | Peak Window | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lavender | Mid-July – early August | Farm Tomita, Lavender East (Furano), Shikisai no Oka (Biei) |
| Other flowers (poppies, lupins, marigolds, sunflowers) | June – August | Furano/Biei area |
| Alpine wildflowers (Daisetsuzan) | Early–mid July (lower elevations), late July (high routes) | Asahidake above treeline |
| Rebun Island alpine flowers | June – early August | July is ideal |
| Yubari/Furano melon | July – August | Peak season |
| Corn | Late July – August | Hokkaido corn comes into season |
| Kegani (hairy crab) | Summer months | Unlike other crabs, best in summer not winter |
| Bafun uni (sea urchin) | May – August | Excellent in July |

## Festivals and Events

- **Sapporo Summer Festival / Odori Beer Garden** — ~July 18 – Aug 13; Japan's largest outdoor beer event; 10,000+ seats across 7 blocks of Odori Park; multiple brewery zones (Sapporo, German, international); food includes zangi, Genghis Khan, grilled corn; runs 12:00–21:00 daily
- **Hokkai Heso Matsuri ("Belly Button Festival"), Furano** — late July (historically July 28–29); participants paint faces on their stomachs and belly-dance in parades; one of Hokkaido's most unusual cultural events; deeply local
- **Otaru Ushio Festival** — late July (historically July 25–27); one of Hokkaido's largest summer festivals; massive street parades and fireworks
- **Nakafurano & Kamifurano Flower Festivals** — local events coinciding with lavender peak; smaller, more local feel
- **Niseko Jazz Festival** — mid-July; international jazz acts in a scenic mountain setting
- **Rishiri Island Climbing Festival** — early July; marks official opening of Mt. Rishiri hiking season

## Weather

- Daytime: 20–25°C; nights: 15–18°C; mountains colder
- **No oppressive humidity** — this is the major draw vs. mainland Japan summer
- Periodic rain fronts — pack a compact waterproof layer
- East Hokkaido (Shiretoko, Nemuro) can be cooler and foggier due to the cold Oyashio Current
- Mountain weather in Daisetsuzan and Shiretoko changes rapidly; proper hiking gear essential

---

## Booking and Crowds

- July is peak season (second-busiest month after August)
- Book flights, hotels, and rental cars **6+ months ahead**
- **Farm Tomita** gets very crowded at peak lavender — arrive early morning or go on a weekday
- **Shiretoko Goko ground trails** require advance reservation for guided ranger program during bear season (June to mid-July); solo hiking typically opens after mid-July — confirm the year's schedule when booking
- **Ryokan and onsen accommodation** in Sounkyo and Lake Akan books fast — prioritize early
