# Tokyo — 2-Night Tail End Stay

**Position in trip:** After Hokkaido. Options to get here:
- Fly CTS → HND (1hr 45min, ~$77–$150 on ANA/JAL), OR
- Shinkansen from Hakodate → Tokyo (Shin-Hakodate-Hokuto → Tokyo, ~4hrs) — if Hakodate is your last Hokkaido stop, this is a great option that skips the CTS → HND flight entirely

Fly home from **HND or NRT → LAS**.

## Restaurant: Cocon

**Full name:** cocon (lowercase)
**Address:** 4F Chiyo Nakameguro Building Annex, 3-8-3 Kami-Meguro, Meguro-ku, Tokyo
**Neighborhood:** Nakameguro — stylish, canal-side residential neighborhood
**Access:** 1–2 min walk from Nakameguro Station (Tokyu Toyoko / Tokyo Metro Hibiya lines)
**Closed:** Wednesdays

### About
An intimate counter-seated fine dining experience. Chef **Masataka Kuriwaki** trained at 3-Michelin-star Mirazur (France), then worked at Amour (Ebisu, 1 Michelin star), and did a significant stint at Bennelong at the Sydney Opera House. The result: French technique with a strong Modern Australian accent and Japanese seasonal ingredients, with natural wine pairing as a core part of the experience. Small number of covers; chef cooks in front of diners at the counter.

### Reservations
- **Pocket Concierge** — primary English-friendly booking platform; listed directly
- **Tabelog** — Japan's dominant restaurant platform; online reservations available
- **AutoReserve** — instant reservation option
- Book **2–4 weeks in advance** for dinner; small venue fills quickly
- **Cancellation policy:** 30% charge 6–7 days prior; 50% charge 2–5 days prior; 80% day before/day of; 100% no-show

### Practical Details
- Dress code: smart casual to business casual
- English menus available; English communication is manageable given the international booking platforms and Australian influence
- Tabelog score: 3.56 (solid — 3.5+ is "good" on Tabelog's scale)
- No current Michelin star, but chef's pedigree is notable (Mirazur 3-star, Amour 1-star)

## Souvenir Shopping

### For Hokkaido-Specific Souvenirs (if you didn't buy everything up there)

**Hokkaido Dosanko Plaza** ⭐ First stop
- **Location:** Tokyo Kotsu Kaikan Building 1F, 2-10-1 Yurakucho, Chiyoda-ku (5 min walk from Ginza; near Hibiya/Yurakucho Station)
- **Hours:** 10:00–22:00
- Official Hokkaido government antenna shop; 1,200+ products exclusively from Hokkaido
- **Best picks:** Royce Nama Chocolate & Potato Chip Chocolate, Rokkatei Marusei Butter Sand (the most iconic Hokkaido confection), pickled salmon, Yubari melon soft serve (in-store), rotating limited-edition products from small Hokkaido producers
- Gets crowded on weekends — go on a weekday morning if possible

### For Traditional Japanese Souvenirs

**Asakusa** — the best single neighborhood for traditional souvenirs
- **Nakamise Dori** — 250m shopping street (Japan's oldest) leading to Sensoji Temple; 89 shops
- Look for: Edo Kiriko cut glass, fans, tenugui hand towels, ceramics, chopsticks, Ningyo-yaki sweets
- **Funawa Honten** — imo-yokan (sweet potato jelly), open since 1902
- **Ame-shin** — hand-sculpted amezaiku candy art; uniquely Tokyo, very giftable

### For High-End / Premium Gifts

**Ginza**
- **Ginza Six** — largest retail complex in Ginza; underground food hall for beautifully packaged Japanese sweets and bento gifts
- **Ginza Mitsukoshi / Matsuya Ginza** — classic department stores; Japan-exclusive products, impeccably wrapped food gifts
- **Shiseido Parlour** — signature Hanatsubaki Biscuits in a black tin, exclusive to the Ginza flagship store

### For Variety / Last-Minute

**Tokyu Hands** (Shibuya flagship) — stationery, health and beauty, kitchenware, uniquely Japanese practical gifts; 7 floors
**Don Quijote (Donki)** — affordable snacks in bulk, quirky goods, cosmetics; open late/24hrs; good for filling out gift bags
**Tokyo Station** (Gransta / Tokyo Character Street) — regional omiyage from across Japan, character goods, Tokyo-exclusive items; ideal if you're short on time before the train or airport

## Logistics

### Getting to/from Airport

**Haneda (HND) — recommended for this trip**
- Tokyo Monorail from Hamamatsucho: ~20 min, ¥500
- Keikyu Line: direct to Shinagawa, connects onward to Shinjuku and Yokohama
- Taxi: ~¥4,000–¥6,000 from central Tokyo

**Narita (NRT)**
- Narita Express (N'EX): ~60 min to Shinjuku, ¥3,070
- Limousine Bus: ~90 min, ¥3,200
- Much further from the city — factor in significant extra travel time on departure day

### Where to Stay
For a 2-night tail stay, **Nakameguro**, **Ginza**, or **Shibuya** are all well-positioned — close to Cocon, good shopping, and easy Haneda connections. If flying out from HND, a hotel near Shinagawa makes departure morning very easy (15 min on the Keikyu Line).
