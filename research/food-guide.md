# Hokkaido Food Guide

Hokkaido is widely considered the best food region in all of Japan — extraordinary seafood from three surrounding seas, exceptional dairy from Japan's largest farming region, and a distinct set of dishes that evolved here.

---

## Ramen — Three Regional Styles

| Style | City | Broth | Noodles | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Miso** | Sapporo | Thick, rich miso | Medium-thick curly | Topped with pork belly, corn, bean sprouts, butter pat |
| **Shio (Salt)** | Hakodate | Clear, light chicken/seafood | Thin, straight | Oldest style; delicate, lets ingredients speak |
| **Shoyu (Soy)** | Asahikawa | Double stock (pork + seafood), soy | Thin, straight | Float of lard on top traps heat |

**Where to eat:**
- **Sapporo Ramen Yokocho (Ramen Alley)** — atmospheric postwar-era alley in Susukino; most famous for miso ramen
- **Menya Saimi (Sapporo)** — considered the gold standard for Sapporo miso ramen; regularly draws long lines of locals; worth the wait
- **Ramen Ichiryuan (Sapporo)** — another well-regarded local option
- **Hakodate Ajisai** — founded 1930; the most historically significant shio ramen shop
- **Asahikawa Ramen Village (Ramen Mura)** — 8 of the most legendary local shops under one roof
- **Ramen Shishio (Asahikawa)** — 15-seat dive hidden off a side street; incredible shoyu ramen; a true hidden gem

## Seafood

Hokkaido is surrounded by three cold seas (Pacific, Sea of Japan, Sea of Okhotsk) — Japan's premier cold-water seafood source.

- **Uni (sea urchin)** — Hokkaido uni is considered Japan's finest. Two varieties: **Bafun uni** (smaller, intensely sweet, orange; peaks May–Aug) and **Murasaki uni** (larger, more delicate, cream-colored). Best as donburi or straight from the shell. **Uni Murakami** has two must-visit locations: the Hakodate branch near the morning market (tiny counter, urchin sourced directly from Rishiri Island) and a Sapporo branch.
- **Ikura (salmon roe)** — large, bursting, intense flavor; appears on virtually every kaisendon
- **Ika (squid)** — particularly in Hakodate; live squid sashimi is a regional specialty; Hakodate Morning Market lets you fish your own
- **Kani (crab)** — three types: **Kegani** (hairy crab, Hokkaido specialty; best in summer), **Tarabagani** (red king crab), **Zuwaigani** (snow crab). **Kani Honke (Sapporo)** is a legendary full-on crab feast restaurant with hairy crabs flown directly from Tomakomai.
- **Hokke (atka mackerel)** — grilled; casual staple in Hokkaido izakayas

**Kaisen-don (seafood rice bowls)** — the defining Hokkaido meal. The "tricolor" bowl (uni, ikura, crab) is the classic.

**Best market experiences:**
- **Hakodate Asaichi (Morning Market)** — opens 5am; the most atmospheric; live squid fishing + immediate preparation
- **Sapporo Nijo Market** — compact; convenient for city visitors; great for breakfast
- **Kushiro Washo Market** — unique "do-it-yourself" kaisendon; buy rice, pile seafood from different vendor stalls

## Dairy

Hokkaido produces ~50% of Japan's milk. Quality is exceptional.

- **Soft-serve ice cream** — everywhere; must-try in every region; vanilla is baseline but cheese, lavender (Furano), and melon flavors are iconic
- **Fresh cheese and butter** — farm shop samples in Furano and Biei; Tokachi region (Obihiro) is the dairy heartland
- **Convenience store milk/lattes** — Hokkaido milk is noticeably richer; 7-Eleven and Lawson milk drinks are genuinely excellent

## Must-Try Dishes

- **Genghis Khan (Jingisukan)** — mutton/lamb grilled on a dome-shaped metal grill at the table; the definitive Hokkaido BBQ; pairs with cold Sapporo beer. Multiple great options in Sapporo: **Sapporo Beer Garden** (classic, historic brick brewery complex), **Jingisukan Daruma Main Branch** (legendary since 1954; fresh mutton, atmospheric), **Royal Park Canvas Rooftop Jingisukan** (rooftop setting, more modern).
- **Soup Curry** — a Sapporo original; spice-forward thin curry broth (not mainland Japanese thick curry) with whole vegetables and protein (classic = chicken leg). **Soup Curry Garaku** (tucked in an alley; considered the "quiet champion") and **Aiiro** are both top picks in Sapporo.
- **Zangi** — Hokkaido fried chicken; more aggressively seasoned than karaage (garlic, soy); ubiquitous at izakayas and summer festivals
- **Hokkaido melon** — **Yubari melon** is Japan's most expensive and famous fruit; **Furano melon** is more accessible and equally delicious; peak season is July–August
- **Corn and potatoes** — Hokkaido produces most of Japan's corn and potatoes; grilled corn on the cob at summer festivals is quintessential; late July is when corn comes into season
- **Obihiro Butadon (pork rice bowl)** — Tokachi/Obihiro specialty; thick slices of pork in sweet tare sauce on rice; simple and deeply satisfying
- **Akkeshi oysters** — the town of Akkeshi on the southeastern coast has Japan's finest oysters, farmed to be excellent year-round including summer (unlike most Japanese oysters which peak in winter); worth a stop if passing through eastern Hokkaido

## Specific Restaurant Picks

### Sapporo
- **Menya Saimi** — gold standard Sapporo miso ramen; locals line up for it
- **Jingisukan Daruma Main Branch** — legendary Genghis Khan since 1954; fresh mutton; atmospheric old-school interior
- **Royal Park Canvas Rooftop Jingisukan** — rooftop Genghis Khan; great views
- **Kani Honke** — full crab feast restaurant; hairy crabs flown from Tomakomai; splurge-worthy
- **Soup Curry Garaku** — the "quiet champion" of Sapporo soup curry, tucked in an alley
- **Aiiro** — another top-rated soup curry spot
- **Okaeri Izakaya (Susukino)** — well-regarded local izakaya in the entertainment district
- **Parfaiteria PaL** — famous parfait café; Sapporo has an obsessive parfait culture (late-night parfaits are a local tradition)
- **Nijo Fish Market / Nijo Ichiba** — breakfast kaisendon; compact and convenient
- **Hokkaido Prefectural Government Office (Red Brick Building)** — the café inside serves an exclusive "red brick sandwich" only available here

### Hakodate
- **Uni Murakami** — tiny counter near the morning market; legendary uni donburi sourced from Rishiri Island
- **Hakodate Asaichi (Morning Market)** — live squid fishing, build-your-own kaisendon; opens 5am

### Asahikawa
- **Ramen Shishio** — 15-seat hidden dive; incredible shoyu ramen; cash only, short hours

### Otaru
- **Kita no Aisu** — hidden gem soft-serve stand operating since 1978, near the canal; velvety Hokkaido milk ice cream

### Jozankei (Onsen Town, near Sapporo)
- **Exclamation Bakery** — a burger restaurant with a foot bath; a quirky and relaxing stop en route to or from Jozankei Onsen

---

## Drinks

- **Sapporo Beer** — brewed here; the classic pairing with Genghis Khan; visit the Sapporo Beer Garden/Museum in the original brick brewery building
- **Hokkaido sake and whisky** — Hokkaido has a growing craft whisky scene; Nikka Whisky's Yoichi Distillery (near Otaru) offers tours and tastings
